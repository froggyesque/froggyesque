Chitin

Chitin is a long-chain polymer of N-acetylglucosamine, a derivative of glucose. It is a primary component in the exoskeletons of arthropods such as crabs, lobsters, and insects, as well as the cell walls of fungi.

In crabs, chitin forms a tough, protective layer that shields them from predators and environmental damage. Beyond its natural role, chitin has many applications, including biodegradable plastics, medical sutures, and water purification materials.
